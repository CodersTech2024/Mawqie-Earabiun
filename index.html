Lesson 1: Arabic Alphabet

Learn the Arabic letters with pronunciation:

  • ا (Alif) - Pronunciation: "A"
  • ب (Baa) - Pronunciation: "B"
  • ت (Taa) - Pronunciation: "T"
  • ث (Thaa) - Pronunciation: "Th"
  • ج (Jeem) - Pronunciation: "J"
  • ح (Haa) - Pronunciation: "H" (soft)
  • خ (Khaa) - Pronunciation: "Kh" (guttural sound)
  • د (Daal) - Pronunciation: "D"
  • ذ (Dhaal) - Pronunciation: "Dh" (as in "this")
  • ر (Raa) - Pronunciation: "R" (rolled)
  • ز (Zaa) - Pronunciation: "Z"
  • س (Seen) - Pronunciation: "S"
  • ش (Sheen) - Pronunciation: "Sh"
  • ص (Saad) - Pronunciation: "S" (emphatic)
  • ض (Daad) - Pronunciation: "D" (emphatic)
  • ط (Taa) - Pronunciation: "T" (emphatic)
  • ظ (Zhaa) - Pronunciation: "Z" (emphatic)
  • ع (Ayn) - Pronunciation: "A" (deep, throat sound)
  • غ (Ghayn) - Pronunciation: "Gh" (guttural)
  • ف (Faa) - Pronunciation: "F"
  • ق (Qaaf) - Pronunciation: "Q" (deep "k" sound)
  • ك (Kaaf) - Pronunciation: "K"
  • ل (Laam) - Pronunciation: "L"
  • م (Meem) - Pronunciation: "M"
  • ن (Noon) - Pronunciation: "N"
  • ه (Haa) - Pronunciation: "H" (as in "hat")
  • و (Waaw) - Pronunciation: "W" or "O" (depending on use)
  • ي (Yaa) - Pronunciation: "Y" or "ee"

Lesson 2: Basic Arabic Grammar

Get familiar with basic grammatical rules in Arabic:

  • Nouns and Gender - Arabic nouns have gender (masculine and feminine). For example:
    • Teacher: Muʿallim (مُعَلِّم) [male], Muʿallimah (مُعَلِّمَة) [female]
    • Student: Talib (طَالِب) [male], Talibah (طَالِبَة) [female]
  • Definite Article - Arabic uses the definite article "ال" (al-) to indicate "the." For example:
    • كتاب (kitab) means "book", while الكتاب (al-kitab) means "the book".
  • Verb Conjugation - Arabic verbs are conjugated based on tense and subject. For example:
    • He writes: هو يكتب (huwa yaktub)
    • She writes: هي تكتب (hiya taktub)
    • I write: أنا أكتب (ana aktub)

Lesson 3: Basic Sentences

Here are some common sentences in Arabic with pronunciation:

  • Hello - السلام عليكم (As-salamu alaykum)
  • How are you? - كيف حالك؟ (Kayfa haluka [male] / Kayfa haluki [female])
  • Thank you - شكراً (Shukran)
  • Yes - نعم (Na'am)
  • No - لا (La)
